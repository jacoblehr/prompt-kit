# Philosophy: Thought Experiment

Apply when an abstract claim, value, or principle needs to be tested against a hypothetical case to reveal its implications or limits.

```text
Design and run a thought experiment to test this principle or claim.

Steps:
1. State the principle or claim to be tested in precise terms
2. Design a hypothetical case that isolates the relevant variable:
   - select a scenario where the principle clearly applies
   - introduce a variation that creates tension or forces a difficult implication
   - strip away distracting real-world complexity
3. Apply the principle rigorously to the hypothetical case
4. Assess the result:
   - is the implied outcome acceptable? If not, what does that reveal about the principle?
   - does the principle need to be qualified, scoped, or revised?
   - what does the thought experiment show that direct argument could not?

Principle or claim:
{paste the claim, rule, or value to examine}
```
