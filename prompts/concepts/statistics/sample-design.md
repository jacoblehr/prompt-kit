# Statistics: Sample Design

Apply when designing a study, survey, poll, or experiment where the quality of the sample determines whether conclusions can be generalised.

```text
Evaluate or design the sample for this study or analysis.

Cover:
- target population: what group are we trying to draw conclusions about?
- sampling frame: what group are we actually sampling from, and how does it differ from the target?
- sampling method: random, stratified, cluster, or convenience — and what biases does it introduce?
- sample size: is it sufficient to detect the effect size that would matter for the decision? (rough power calculation)
- non-response and attrition: who is least likely to respond or remain in the sample, and does that bias the result?
- external validity: to what extent can results be generalised beyond the sample?

Identify the single biggest threat to the validity of conclusions from this sample.

Study or analysis:
{paste description of what is being studied and how the sample is or will be collected}
```
