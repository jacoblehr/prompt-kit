# Statistics: Survivorship Bias

Apply when drawing conclusions from a sample that may only contain successes, completions, or survivors — and the excluded cases would change the conclusion.

```text
Check this analysis for survivorship bias.

Ask:
- what cases are present in the sample? (what made it in)
- what cases are absent? (what was filtered out before observation)
- why were the absent cases excluded? (failure, dropout, inaccessibility, or selection)
- if the absent cases were included, how would the pattern change?
- is the conclusion being drawn only valid for the observed subset, or is it
  being generalised to a broader population?

Identify: what would we need to examine to correct for this?

Analysis or claim:
{paste the study, argument, case, or pattern you are reviewing}
```
