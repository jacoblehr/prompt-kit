# Economics: Sunk Cost

Apply when a past investment (money, time, or effort) is influencing a forward-looking decision in a way that distorts it.

```text
Apply sunk cost analysis to this decision.

Reframe the decision as follows:
- identify what has already been spent (the sunk cost)
- state clearly: that cost cannot be recovered regardless of the choice ahead
- restate the decision purely in terms of future costs and future benefits
- given only the forward-looking view, what is the right choice?
- if the answer changed once sunk costs were excluded, name what was distorting it

Additional check:
- is there a legitimate forward-looking reason to stay the course?
  (learning value, relationship capital, strategic positioning)
- if yes, name it explicitly and separate it from the sunk cost argument

Decision or situation:
{paste the situation and the past investment involved}
```
