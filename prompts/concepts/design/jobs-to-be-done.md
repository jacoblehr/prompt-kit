# Design: Jobs to Be Done

Apply when designing or improving a product, service, or intervention to surface the underlying progress users are trying to make — not just what they do or ask for.

```text
Apply jobs-to-be-done analysis to this product, feature, or user behaviour.

Identify the job:
- what progress is the user trying to make in their life or work?
- what is being hired to do this job now (the current solution, workaround, or non-consumption)?
- what functional job are they trying to accomplish?
- what emotional job are they trying to accomplish? (how do they want to feel?)
- what social job are they trying to accomplish? (how do they want to be seen?)

Examine the context (the "when" and "where"):
- what situation triggers the hire?
- what pushes them away from the current solution?
- what pulls them toward a new solution?
- what anxieties and habits are holding them in place?

Implication:
- what does a solution look like that does this job better than the current alternative?

Product, feature, or behaviour:
{paste what you are analysing}
```
