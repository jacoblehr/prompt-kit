# Triage The Unknown

Use when a situation feels confusing because facts, interpretations, and assumptions are getting mixed together.

```text
Triage this ambiguous situation before trying to solve it.

Separate:
- observed facts: what is directly known or evidenced
- interpretations: what story is being told about those facts
- assumptions: what is currently being taken for granted
- unknowns: what is genuinely missing

Then produce:
- the two or three most plausible explanations
- the single confusion that matters most to resolve first
- the smallest discriminating check or question that would collapse uncertainty fastest
- what not to do yet because it would commit too early

Situation:
{paste the symptoms, questions, observations, and available evidence}
```
