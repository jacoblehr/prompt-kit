# Argument Structure

Use when you need to understand the logical anatomy of a claim before evaluating or responding to it.

```text
Extract and map the logical structure of this argument.

Identify:
- the main conclusion (what the author wants you to accept)
- the premises (stated reasons given to support the conclusion)
- any hidden or unstated premises the argument depends on
- the logical form: is the conclusion deductively entailed, or only made probable?
- any key definitions or distinctions the argument relies on

Then assess:
- which premise is doing the most structural work?
- which premise is most contestable?
- does the conclusion follow given the premises, or is there a gap?

Argument:
{paste argument, essay, position, or chain of reasoning}
```
