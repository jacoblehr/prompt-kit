# Persuasion Audit

Use when you need to assess how persuasive a piece of writing is and identify where it loses the reader.

```text
Audit the persuasive strength of the following piece of writing.

Text: {text}

Intended audience: {audience}

Goal (what the writing should make the reader believe or do): {goal}

Evaluate:
1. Opening — does it establish why the reader should care? How quickly?
2. Claim clarity — is the central claim stated plainly and early?
3. Evidence quality — is the evidence credible, specific, and sufficient for the claim?
4. Objection handling — are the strongest counter-arguments addressed honestly?
5. Logical structure — does each section follow from the last? Are there gaps or leaps?
6. Emotional register — is the tone appropriate for the audience and goal?
7. Call to action — is it clear what the reader should believe or do after reading?

Deliver:
- Overall persuasion score: Strong / Adequate / Weak, with one sentence of justification
- The single biggest reason a sceptical reader would not be convinced
- Three specific edits that would most improve persuasive impact
```
