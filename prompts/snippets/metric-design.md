# Metric Design

Use when you need to design a measure for an abstract concept — product quality, team health, customer satisfaction, or any outcome that is not directly observable.

```text
Design a metric for the following concept.

Concept to measure: {concept}

Decision this metric will inform: {decision}

Constraints (data available, measurement frequency, audience): {constraints}

Address:
1. Operationalisation — the specific, observable quantity that will stand in for the concept
2. Validity — how well does the metric actually capture what you care about, and where does it diverge?
3. Goodhart risk — how could this metric be gamed or optimised in a way that destroys its signal?
4. Leading vs lagging — does this measure a cause or an effect? What is the lag?
5. Benchmarks — what would good, acceptable, and bad values look like?
6. Counter-metric — what should move in the opposite direction if the primary metric improves deceptively?

Produce a one-paragraph metric specification: what is measured, how, how often, and how it will be used.
```
