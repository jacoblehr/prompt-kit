# Correlation Vs Causation

Use when an observed association is being used to justify a causal claim or a policy decision.

```text
Assess whether a causal claim is justified from this observed association.

Steps:
1. State the association precisely: what varies with what?
2. Test reverse causation: could the effect be causing the cause instead?
3. Test confounding: is there a third variable that causes both?
4. Apply the Bradford Hill criteria where applicable:
   - strength of association
   - consistency across studies and populations
   - temporality (cause precedes effect)
   - dose-response relationship
   - plausibility (a credible mechanism exists)
5. What experiment would most efficiently establish or refute the causal direction?

Verdict: is the claim well-supported, plausible but requiring more evidence, or unjustified?

Also note: what decision would change if causation is not established?

Claim and supporting data:
{paste the association, the data, and the causal claim being made}
```
