# Second-Order Effects

Use when a decision or action may trigger consequences beyond the obvious first-order outcomes.

```text
Map the second and third-order effects of this action or decision.

Requirements:
- first: state the intended or obvious first-order effect
- second: for each first-order effect, name what it causes in turn
- third: trace at least one chain two levels deeper
- flag any effects that contradict the intended outcome (backfire risk)
- flag any effects that disproportionately impact a group not currently in view
- identify the most likely unintended consequence and its magnitude

Action or decision:
{paste action or decision}

Context:
{paste relevant context, constraints, or system in which this takes place}
```

Domain tags:
- systems thinking
- critical thinking
- risk management
