# Delegation Brief

Use when handing off a task to ensure the person receiving it has everything they need to succeed without coming back for clarification.

```text
Write a delegation brief for this task.

Include:
- the goal: what done looks like, not just what to do
- context: why this matters and how it connects to the larger objective
- scope: what is in and out of scope — what decisions they can make without checking back
- constraints: non-negotiables (timing, budget, quality bar, style, stakeholders to involve)
- resources: what they have access to and who they can ask
- check-ins: when and how to sync (not how often — when it is actually needed)
- success signal: how you will both know the task is complete and good enough

Avoid describing how to do the work. Focus on what is needed and what good looks like.

Task:
{paste task description}

Context about the person receiving it (optional):
{paste any relevant context about their background or current workload}
```
