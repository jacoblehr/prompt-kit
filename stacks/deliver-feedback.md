# Stack: Deliver Feedback

Use when you need to prepare and structure substantive feedback — on work, a decision, a proposal, or a performance — so it is specific, fair, and actionable.

Useful inputs:

- the work, output, or situation you are giving feedback on
- the person receiving the feedback and your relationship to them
- the goal: improvement, decision, or development

Suggested blocks:

1. `guardrail.blind-spot-check`
2. `frame.feedback-request`
3. `frame.critique-argument`
4. `frame.rewrite-for-clarity`

Expected outcome:

- your own blind spots and potential biases named before structuring the feedback
- feedback request structured: what specifically should the recipient focus on?
- substance critiqued: specific strengths and weaknesses identified with evidence, not impressions
- feedback rewritten for clarity: plain, specific, and constructive — no vague qualifiers

Domain tags:

## Composition notes

**Minimum blocks:** `frame.blind-spot-check`, `frame.critique-argument`

**Why this order works:** Blind-spot-check comes first because the quality of feedback depends on whether the giver's own biases have been named. Feedback-request then structures exactly what the recipient should focus on. Critique-argument provides the substantive evaluation surface. Rewrite-for-clarity is the final pass — feedback that is accurate but unclear or vague fails at the delivery stage.

**Common swaps:** Swap `frame.critique-argument` for `mode.critique` + `frame.code-review` for software feedback specifically. Swap `frame.rewrite-for-clarity` for `rubric.writing-quality` when the feedback is written and will be read without a follow-up conversation.

**Common failure mode:** Skipping the blind-spot check. Feedback without giver self-awareness tends to address the giver's preferences rather than the recipient's actual development needs.
