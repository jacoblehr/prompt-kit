# Steelman

Use when:

- you are about to reject or override a position
- you want to ensure you are defeating the best version of an argument, not a weaker caricature
- you suspect your critique may be motivated rather than rigorous

Helps prevent:

- attacking straw versions of opposing views
- overconfident dismissal of alternatives
- blind spots in your own position

How to use it:

1. state the position you are considering challenging
2. argue it as strongly as possible — as its best proponent would
3. identify what evidence or reasoning would genuinely defeat that strongest version
4. proceed with critique only once you have met that bar

Quick invocation:

```text
Apply the STEELMAN strategy.
Argue the strongest version of the position I am considering challenging.
Make the case as well as its best proponent would.
Then identify what would actually be required to defeat that strongest version.
```
